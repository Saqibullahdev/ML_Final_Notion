Problem: Choosing the Right Model

You're working as a data scientist at a company that maintains hundreds of industrial machines. The company recently experienced unexpected machine failures, and management wants to build a system to predict future failures.

You are given the following data:

  • Logs from 5 years of machine operations (temperature, vibration levels, performance metrics).
  • About 50,000 records of normal machine behavior.
  • Only 20 records of machine failure events.

You are asked to choose between Anomaly Detection and Supervised Learning to build a model that alerts the team when a machine may fail.

Task for Students:

  1. Analyze the nature of the data (number of positive and negative examples).
  1. Decide whether to use Anomaly Detection or Supervised Learning.
  1. Justify your choice using concepts like training example balance, generalization, and future data behavior.

✅ Solution:

Model to Use: Anomaly Detection

Justification:

  • There is a very small number of positive examples (only 20 failures) and a large number of negative examples (normal operation).
  • Future machine failures might look different from past ones.
  • This fits the characteristics of Anomaly Detection:
    • Few positive examples (y = 1).
    • Many negative examples (y = 0).
    • High uncertainty in what future anomalies will look like.

Supervised Learning is not suitable here because it requires enough positive examples for the model to learn what a failure looks like, and that condition is not met in this problem.

Problem: Choosing the Right Model

You are working with a smart agriculture startup building an AI system to predict crop disease based on drone images and sensor data from fields.

You are given:

  • 20,000 labeled records, each tagged as either “healthy crop” or “infected crop”.
  • Each record includes:
    • Multispectral drone images.
    • Soil moisture levels.
    • Leaf color metrics.
    • Temperature/humidity data.
  • About 30% of the crops in the dataset are infected.
  • The infected crops show recognizable patterns (e.g., color shifts, moisture loss).
  • Agronomists confirm future infections are likely to follow similar biological indicators.

Your job is to build a model that helps farmers detect infections early and apply targeted treatment.

Task for Students:

  1. Review the data distribution and feature structure.
  1. Choose between Anomaly Detection and Supervised Learning.
  1. Provide a clear justification using machine learning principles.

✅ Solution:

Model to Use: Supervised Learning

Justification:

  • The dataset has a substantial number of labeled examples for both infected and healthy crops.
  • The infected crop examples are not extremely rare (30%) and follow consistent, learnable patterns.
  • Future infected crops are expected to look similar to the training data, making generalization feasible.
  • The structured features (images + sensor data) are ideal for training a supervised model to classify the crops accurately.

Anomaly Detection would not be appropriate here because:

  • The imbalance is not extreme.
  • Infected crops are well-represented and labeled.
  • The goal is classification, not spotting unknown rare events.
